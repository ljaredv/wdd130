Perú Piura

Piura is a vibrant city in northwestern Peru known as the "City of Eternal Heat" for its year-round tropical climate. Founded by Francisco Pizarro in 1532, it is the oldest Spanish city in South America. It serves as a bustling commercial hub and the gateway to world-famous northern beaches like Máncora.
